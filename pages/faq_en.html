The rumor that GUE prescribes the use of a specific brand persists, but it is false. There is a specific equipment configuration that must be adhered to during the course, regardless of the brand, and its advantages will be explained to you during the course. The sidemount configuration will also be discussed in detail. But here, too, there are various brands to choose from.

The costs are listed in detail under “Prices.” The daily rate does not include registration fees for GUE and certification fees for SDI / TDI. There are also costs for gases, rental equipment (if applicable), and €10 per day for the use of Deepstop’s facilities, as well as lake entrance fees where applicable. Should the course take place in a distant location, the instructor’s expenses such as travel and accommodation will be charged.

We meet at 8:30 a.m. at Deepstop and start the day with theory. Christof will tailor the lesson to your situation and prior knowledge rather than following a fixed program. After a break, he will check your equipment and help you optimize it if necessary. This is followed by dry runs, allowing you to practice all skills on land. Afterwards, you will drive to a nearby lake and do 1-2 dives. Finally, there will be a detailed debriefing.

Your equipment should be ready to use as far as possible. You can fill (or rent) your tanks on site. Otherwise, just bring a positive attitude! After all, diving should be fun.

Christof himself dives all year round, of course. However, courses don’t make much sense when it’s too cold. Experience has shown that concentration levels drop too much and you can’t get the most out of the course. It’s also helpful to be able to feel your fingers when learning skills. This is especially true for sidemount. All courses are only held in local waters and never under the easier but unrealistic conditions of an indoor dive center. Therefore, courses usually take place between April and November.

In the old GUE course system, you could upgrade after completing a Provisional or Rec Pass. You received feedback during the course, practiced, and returned for a type of test dive. If everything went well, you received a higher rating (Rec Pass or Tec Pass). The new course structure provides a clearer distinction between technical diving and recreational diving. That’s why the new Performance Diver and Basic Fundamentals courses were created. Technical Fundamentals, on the other hand, is aimed at divers who want to get into technical diving or who already dive technically and want to improve their skills. It is still possible to progress from Basic Fundamentals to Technical Fundamentals. However, this requires making up for the missing course content, which means at least one additional day and two dives. The various options can be found in detail here: https://www.gue.com/files/Standards_and_Procedures/GUE-Standards-v10.1_Student-FAQs.pdf
